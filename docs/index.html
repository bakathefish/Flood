In August 2025 Punjab suffered its worst flood since 1988 — all 23 districts, ~3.55 lakh people, 1.7 lakh hectares of crops. The satellite maps that could have guided relief existed, but locked in PDFs. Sailaab rebuilds the capability in the open: SAR flood mapping, a decade hazard atlas, damage analytics, a district flood forecaster, and a live monitor — every line of code and every number public.

The System

Five modules, one pipeline

  • Atlas — Sentinel-1 change detection + Random Forest, per-district statistics
  • Decade hazard — 11 monsoons → recurrence zones → "repeat victims" table
  • Impact — crop-flooded hectares, exposure, the dam-release causal chain
  • Forecaster — XGBoost district risk model trained on the pipeline's own decade of SAR-derived labels
  • Live monitor — every new satellite pass → district damage → trilingual alerts, automatically

Built entirely on open, login-free data: Copernicus Sentinel-1, Copernicus GFM, IMD rainfall, CWC reservoir records, ISRO NDEM validation. Anyone can reproduce every number.

Engineering honesty

Every satellite step is gated by a pre-declared checkpoint — the expected number is written down before the run, then the actual, then PASS or FAIL. The failures stay in the log.

Training labels are bootstrapped from method agreement and labeled as such. Both spatial folds are reported. The June "floods" that turned out to be rice transplanting? Documented, calibrated, published.

161 automated tests. MIT-licensed code, CC-BY data products. Method paper, data-source registry and verification log live in the repository docs.
