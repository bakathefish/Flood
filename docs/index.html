In August 2025 Punjab suffered its worst flood since 1988: all 23 districts, ~3.55 lakh people, 1.48–1.75 lakh hectares of crops (official range). India's flood-forecast network had zero stations in the state, and the satellite maps that could have guided relief sat locked inside PDFs. Sailaab rebuilds the capability in the open: SAR flood mapping, a decade hazard atlas, damage analytics, a district flood forecaster and a live monitor, with every line of code and every number public.

The proof: 2025, held out

The live board above runs the same model you see tested here. We trained it only on 2015 to 2024, then handed it 2025, the worst flood since 1988, and let the year grade it.

Trained on 2015–2024 only → asked about 2025 · peak P in the pre-declared Aug 14–Sep 12 flood windows
✓ = district actually flooded in Aug–Sep 2025. All five real flood districts rank on top; every other district scores ≤ 0.27. Our satellite damage ranking also matches the government's ground survey: Spearman ρ = 0.72 across all 20 districts (0.56 over the 16 officially named) vs the Special Girdawari table. Full tables: data/forecaster_2025_hindcast.csv, data/official_relief_2025.csv.
~10 days of lead: by the Aug 14–24 window, before the Aug 26–27 dam releases, four of the five eventual flood districts were already in the model's statewide top-5. SHAP says antecedent flooding and the flood-history prior dominate, with Bhakra storage at #3. We ablation-tested that: delete every dam feature and the 2025 flags don't move (data/forecaster_ablation.csv).

05

The Evidence

A different sensor says yes

237 photo-interpreted Sentinel-2 optical truth points (a different satellite with different physics, so no SAR-chain circularity) confirm 81.7% of RF flood pixels as standing water two weeks post-peak. The pre-declared gate was 60%. Every pre-declared precision and recall ordering across Tier-A, RF and GFM held.

We also benchmarked a deep-learning U-Net (Sen1Floods11): test IoU 0.63, but on Punjab it under-segments badly (recall 0.24 vs GFM). Our simpler stack beat the imported deep model, and we published that result instead of hiding it.

The people in the water

GHSL population under the mapped 2025 water: 0.76–1.78 lakh people (RF → GFM extents, conservation-checked to 99.6–100%) against the official 3.55 lakh "affected". The gap is the explanation: flooded land is overwhelmingly low-density cropland, 146–206 people/km² versus Punjab's ~550 mean. "Affected" reaches beyond the waterline; our number is the in-water floor.

Every satellite claim above is gated by a pre-declared numeric band in docs/VERIFICATION-LOG.md: expectation written before the run, actual after, PASS or FAIL shipped either way.
06

The System

Five modules, one pipeline

  • Atlas: Sentinel-1 change detection + Random Forest, per-district statistics, submergence-duration mapping
  • Decade hazard: 11 monsoons → recurrence zones → 91 tehsils scored → the named "repeat victims" table
  • Impact: crop-flooded hectares, the ₹351–523 crore damage band (duration-weighted → at-risk), population exposure, dam headroom, the causal chain
  • Forecaster: XGBoost district risk model trained on the pipeline's own decade of SAR-derived labels, SHAP-explained, ablation-stress-tested
  • Live monitor: every new satellite pass → district damage → trilingual alerts + live model risk (nowcast), automatically

Built entirely on open, login-free data: Copernicus Sentinel-1, Copernicus GFM, IMD rainfall, CWC reservoir records, ISRO NDEM validation. Anyone can reproduce every number.

Engineering honesty

Every satellite step is gated by a pre-declared checkpoint: the expected number is written down before the run, then the actual, then PASS or FAIL. The log ships in the repo, failures included.

Training labels are bootstrapped from method agreement and labeled as such. Both spatial folds are reported. The June "floods" that turned out to be rice transplanting are documented, calibrated and published. And we ran an ablation on ourselves: delete the dam features and the 2025 flags don't move, while a persistence baseline nearly matches pooled precision. We published that too.

408 automated tests, written before the code they test. MIT-licensed code, CC-BY data products. Method paper, data-source registry and verification log live in the repository docs.
